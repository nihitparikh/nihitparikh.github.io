1.5 mi 50 ft Easy 1 hr

3.8 mi 1,200 ft Moderate 2.5 hrs

5 mi 900 ft Easy–Mod 3 hrs

3 mi 300 ft Easy 1.5 hrs

🌊 Narada Falls Viewpoint
0.2 mi Flat Easy 15 min
168-foot waterfall visible from the roadside pullout. A quick stop on the way to Paradise that gets its own "wow" moment. Great for photos.

What This Trip Feels Like
"Standing in a wildflower meadow with a 14,411-foot volcano filling the entire sky — no photo prepares you for this."
🌸July is wildflower peak — the Paradise meadows are otherworldly in early July, with every color of alpine bloom carpeting the hillsides.
🎯Simple, satisfying structure: one mountain, four perspectives, zero driving confusion. You wake up knowing exactly what you're doing.
⚠️Nightlife is minimal. Packwood is a mountain town with a great brewery but it's not a bar-hopping scene. July 4th in Tacoma/Seattle compensates well.
📸The photography is absolutely elite. Every person in your group will take a photo that becomes their phone wallpaper for a year.
🥾Trails are accessible to moderate hikers. The biggest challenge is parking — arrive early (7 AM or before) and this is a breeze.

What This Trip Feels Like
"In one weekend you go from standing in a mountain meadow at 5,700 feet to walking behind a Pacific sea arch with waves crashing around you. No other park does this."
🌊The Pacific coastline is genuinely unlike anything in the other two options. Sea stacks, driftwood, tide pools — it satisfies the beach craving while being dramatically different from a typical beach vacation.
🌿The Hoh Rainforest stops every single person cold. Even the group skeptics who "don't care about nature" go quiet when they walk into that ancient moss cathedral.
⚠️The two-base logistics add complexity. Moving from Port Angeles to Forks takes time and mental energy — budget accordingly and keep days 1–3 lighter on driving.
🎆July 4th fireworks over Port Angeles Harbor is genuinely stunning — Pacific Northwest setting, military parade, harbor fireworks. One of the best-kept July 4th secrets in the country.
🥾This is the friendliest park for non-hikers. The most iconic experiences (Hall of Mosses, Ruby Beach) are flat and require almost no fitness. Everyone participates equally.

What This Trip Feels Like
"Your group does the first July 4th parade in America at midnight, sleeps in a cabin hot tub, sees wild elk in an ancient Appalachian valley, and walks behind a 80-foot waterfall. This is the most complete trip of the three."

🍺The food and nightlife are genuinely excellent for a mountain town. Ole Red, Sugarlands, Jason Aldean's bar, the Gatlinburg strip — your group has options every night without driving.

⚠️The Smokies doesn't have the visual drama of Rainier or the oceanic diversity of Olympic. It's lush and green rather than volcanic and glacial. Some hikers find it "too forested" — manage expectations.
💰Cleanest logistics and best value. One base, easy flights, free park entry, group cabin. You spend less time managing logistics and more time having fun.

For your group: Great Smoky Mountains wins on points
You described wanting hiking + food/nightlife + beach/water, with a West Coast/Southwest departure and $900–$1,500/person budget. You're a non-heavy hiking group celebrating July 4th weekend.

The Smokies delivers on 5 of your 6 stated priorities (the only miss: no ocean beach).
